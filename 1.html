The Challenge: The BFS Cohort

A significant cohort of students with outstanding fees (BFS) are unable to re-enrol each year. Our traditional support model has been reactive, relying on students booking time-intensive, one-on-one appointments.

  • Creates a bottleneck for senior advisor time.
  • Risks losing at-risk students who disengage before seeking help.

The Goal: To develop a proactive, scalable system to identify and support these students more efficiently.

The Solution: An Automated Triage Tool

To solve this, I developed an automated triage tool, leveraging modern technologies to shift our approach from reactive to proactive. As part of my role in finding innovative solutions, I used AI as a co-development partner to rapidly build and refine the automation.

Triage Model Accuracy: High Precision & Key Learnings

High-Precision Prioritisation

4 out of 4 students (100%) who scored highly and were contacted had viable CPR cases. The system correctly prioritised every high-scoring respondent.

System Resilience & Data Quality

2 additional students with viable cases were identified despite scoring lower, due to incorrect or incomplete answers on the form.

Key Finding

The triage logic is sound. The 2 outliers represent a data quality issue, not a system flaw, proving the tool works as designed.

Strategic Insights: What We Learned

Insight 1: Proactive Intervention is Key

This pilot targeted ~60 students in Bad Financial Standing (BFS) at the end of the academic year due to outstanding fees. Proving the model's success with this specific cohort validates its potential as a proactive tool to be deployed earlier, catching students during the academic year as soon as funding issues arise.

Insight 2: The Triage Model is a Definitive Success

The model's ability to correctly filter out 21 non-viable cases is a major efficiency win. It validates the tool as a reliable method for focusing advisor resources.

Insight 3: Form Design is Critical

The 2 outliers caused by incorrect answers highlight the need to refine future forms with clearer questions and validation to improve data quality.
